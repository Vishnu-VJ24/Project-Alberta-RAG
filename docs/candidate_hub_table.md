# Candidate Hub Table

Date: 2026-06-29

## Purpose

This document lists the five candidate tourism and recreation hubs identified for the Bow and Red Deer River corridors. Each hub is described using the eight-criteria screening framework developed in Week 1. The table is the first structured output connecting TDZ alignment, corridor geography, transportation access, economic potential, visitor markets, and research evidence.

This document will be updated as new data, geospatial analysis, and stakeholder input become available.

---

## Hub 1 — Rocky Mountain House / David Thompson: West-Central Adventure Gateway

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Hub Name** | West-Central Adventure Gateway |
| **Anchor Community** | Rocky Mountain House |
| **TDZ Alignment** | David Thompson TDZ |
| **Corridor Connection** | Upper Red Deer River corridor; Clearwater River confluence zone; western entry point to Rocky Mountain foothills |
| **Main Highways / Access Routes** | Hwy 11 (David Thompson Hwy, east–west link from Red Deer to Nordegg and Banff National Park); Hwy 22 (north–south foothills spine); Hwy 54 (link to Red Deer) |
| **Key Activities** | Wildlife viewing, backcountry cabins, mountain biking, hiking, fishing, kayaking/canoe, heritage sites, scenic touring, cross-country skiing |
| **Economic Potential Indicators** | 1,838 potential jobs; 7.2% annual visitor spend growth; $252M estimated tourism spend growth (highest annual growth rate of all reviewed TDZs) |
| **Target Visitor Markets** | Calgary day-trippers and weekend visitors; Edmonton weekend travelers; outdoor adventure seekers; heritage and culture visitors; international travelers extending Banff trips |
| **Transportation Gaps** | No scheduled transit from Calgary or Edmonton; Hwy 11 seasonality and road condition issues in winter; limited last-mile access to backcountry sites; no airport nearby; shuttle/vanpool service absent |
| **Data Still Needed** | Seasonal visitor counts by month; accommodation capacity and occupancy rates; trail use data; origin-destination data for Hwy 11 travelers; Indigenous tourism partnership inventory |
| **RAG Source Documents Needed** | David Thompson TDZ discovery page; Parks Canada visitor data (Abraham Lake, Clearwater); Rocky Mountain House NHS visitor records; Hwy 11 traffic counts; municipal tourism plans for Rocky Mountain House and Clearwater County |

| **Corridor Connection** | Upper Bow River corridor; foothills gateway west and north of Calgary; main dispersal zone for Calgary-origin day-trip and weekend visitors |

## Hub 5 — Drumheller / Canadian Badlands: Red Deer River Badlands Hub

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Hub Name** | Red Deer River Badlands Hub |
| **Anchor Community** | Drumheller |
| **TDZ Alignment** | Canadian Badlands TDZ |
| **Corridor Connection** | Lower Red Deer River corridor; eastern terminus of the corridor analysis; bridge between prairie landscape and river valley tourism |
| **Main Highways / Access Routes** | Hwy 9 (east from Calgary through Drumheller); Hwy 56 (north–south Red Deer River valley link); Hwy 27 (north–west link to Sundre and Red Deer); Hwy 36 (southern link to Hanna and prairie interior); Hwy 10 (Drumheller valley loop) |
| **Key Activities** | Dinosaur paleontology, Royal Tyrrell Museum, hiking and badlands walks, camping, scenic drives, canyon viewpoints, bird watching, photography, dark sky viewing, heritage murals, Indigenous interpretive sites |
| **Economic Potential Indicators** | 1,340 potential jobs; 4.5% annual visitor spend growth; $186M estimated tourism spend growth; 83% of residents welcome more visitors (highest resident support of all reviewed TDZs) |
| **Target Visitor Markets** | Calgary families (Drumheller is a top day-trip from Calgary); international paleontology and dinosaur-focused visitors; school group and educational travelers; US road-trip travelers; photographers and nature documentarians; heritage and culture visitors |
| **Transportation Gaps** | No transit between Calgary and Drumheller; seasonal road closures in valley; limited accommodation capacity for peak summer demand; no airport; last-mile access to remote badlands viewpoints and hoodoos reliant on personal vehicle |
| **Data Still Needed** | Royal Tyrrell Museum annual visitation data; Calgary-origin proportion of Drumheller visitors; accommodation capacity and occupancy; river valley trail usage; Indigenous cultural tourism operator inventory |
| **RAG Source Documents Needed** | Canadian Badlands TDZ discovery page; Royal Tyrrell Museum annual report; Drumheller Valley tourism plan; Government of Alberta provincial park data (Dinosaur Provincial Park); Statistics Canada travel survey for prairie region; Indigenous cultural tourism association data |
