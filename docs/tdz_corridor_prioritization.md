# TDZ Corridor Prioritization Note

Date: 2026-06-24

## Purpose

This note identifies the Tourism Development Zones (TDZs) most relevant to the Bow and Red Deer River corridor project and compares their early strategic fit using Travel Alberta's public TDZ discovery pages.

## Why This Matters

The project needs to recommend tourism hubs and transportation improvements that align with Alberta's existing tourism development framework. TDZ alignment gives the final report a stronger policy foundation and helps the RAG system answer questions about why specific regions were prioritized.

## Initial Findings

- The Travel Alberta TDZ framework is directly relevant because the discovery pages frame TDZs as areas with tourism growth and economic impact potential.
- The project should not treat the Bow and Red Deer corridors as one uniform geography. The corridor appears to break into western adventure access zones, central rural market/service zones, and eastern badlands/cultural landscape zones.
- Foothills has the largest listed spend-growth potential among the reviewed pages, while David Thompson has the highest annual visitor spend growth rate among the reviewed pages.
- Olds to Lacombe appears especially important for Central Alberta because it includes a strong business engagement base and activities that fit rural tourism development.
- Cochrane to Sundre appears important for Bow corridor strategy because it can connect Calgary-region visitor demand with foothills and mountain-adjacent recreation.
- Canadian Badlands appears important for the Red Deer River corridor because it combines river valley landscapes, paleontology, hiking, camping, and strong resident support for increased visitation.
- Resident support matters for implementation. All reviewed TDZ pages report majority support for welcoming more visitors, but the final report should still consider local capacity, infrastructure pressure, and quality-of-life impacts.

## Implications for Hub Planning

The first hub-screening model should include:

- Tourism growth potential from TDZ pages.
- Corridor access and transportation connectivity.
- Fit with top visitor activities listed by Travel Alberta.
- Local business and stakeholder engagement.
- Resident support for increased visitation.
- Ability to reduce regional and seasonal compression.

## Suggested Candidate Hub Themes

- West-central adventure gateway: wildlife viewing, cabins, scenic touring, heritage stops, and river/mountain access.
- Central Alberta rural experience hub: farmers' markets, breweries/distilleries, camping, local food, and small-town itineraries.
- Bow corridor foothills hub: hiking, cabins, day trips from Calgary, and shoulder-season recreation.
- Red Deer River badlands/cultural hub: paleontology, scenic drives, river valley viewpoints, heritage tourism, and overnight touring.
