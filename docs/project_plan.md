# Project Plan

## Working Title

Evidence-based tourism corridor strategy for the Bow and Red Deer River corridors in Central Alberta, supported by a retrieval-augmented generation system.

## Project Thesis

Central Alberta can reduce seasonal and geographic tourism imbalance by developing a small set of connected, evidence-backed tourism hubs along the Bow and Red Deer river corridors. The research will identify under-served zones, transportation constraints, tourism demand signals, demographic shifts, and likely economic impacts. The RAG system will make the evidence base searchable and usable for municipalities, tourism operators, transportation partners, and summit stakeholders.

## Research Questions

1. What tourism trends, opportunities, and threats are most relevant to the Bow and Red Deer River corridors?
2. Which communities or zones are under-served relative to tourism demand, assets, and transportation access?
3. What transportation improvements would most improve visitor access and corridor connectivity?
4. Which recreation adventure hubs have the strongest feasibility and economic impact potential?
5. How do Alberta Tourism Development Zones and the provincial tourism strategy align with corridor development?
6. What marketing and branding strategy would attract visitors while supporting rural communities?
7. How can a RAG system help stakeholders explore the evidence, assumptions, and recommendations?

## Eight-Week Timeline

### Week 1: Scope, Evidence Base, and RAG Skeleton

- Define project geography: Bow corridor, Red Deer corridor, municipalities, highways, parks, attractions, and relevant TDZs.
- Build literature review matrix.
- Collect core public sources from Travel Alberta, Government of Alberta, Statistics Canada, municipal tourism plans, transportation plans, and academic tourism/economic-impact literature.
- Define RAG corpus schema and citation requirements.
- Create first working prototype using a small document set.

### Week 2: Tourism Trends and Baseline Indicators

- Analyze visitor spend, visitation trends, seasonality, hotel performance, air access, and visitor characteristics.
- Build a baseline tourism profile for Central Alberta and relevant TDZs.
- Summarize demand-side opportunities and risks.
- Add cleaned source notes to the RAG corpus.

### Week 3: Geospatial Asset and Gap Analysis

- Map tourism assets: parks, trails, water access, accommodations, events, airports, rail, highways, and visitor services.
- Identify under-served zones using proximity/access metrics and tourism-asset density.
- Produce preliminary maps and gap tables.
- Add GIS metadata and map descriptions to the RAG corpus.

### Week 4: Transportation Needs Assessment

- Analyze road, airport, transit, shuttle, rail, active transportation, and last-mile access gaps.
- Rank improvements by visitor utility, feasibility, cost category, and equity/community benefit.
- Align findings with the Integrated Transportation Summit themes.
- Prepare a transportation improvement plan draft.

### Week 5: Recreation Adventure Hub Scenarios

- Define 3-5 candidate hub concepts.
- Estimate demand fit, infrastructure needs, environmental constraints, partnership needs, and seasonality benefits.
- Use comparable case studies from similar tourism corridor projects.
- Add scenario evidence to RAG.

### Week 6: Economic Impact Model

- Build an economic-impact framework using visitor spending assumptions, direct/indirect/induced effects, jobs, tax revenue, and sensitivity ranges.
- Document assumptions clearly.
- Compare scenarios under conservative, base, and high-growth cases.
- Generate charts and tables for the final report.

### Week 7: Marketing, Branding, and Stakeholder Materials

- Draft marketing strategy for rural visitor dispersal, shoulder-season travel, outdoor recreation, cultural tourism, and transportation-linked itineraries.
- Prepare summit-facing materials: executive brief, findings deck outline, and RAG demo prompts.
- Validate claims against evidence and citations.

### Week 8: Final Report and RAG Evaluation

- Finalize full report.
- Evaluate RAG quality: citation accuracy, answer faithfulness, source coverage, and failure modes.
- Create final demo dataset and stakeholder question set.
- Package deliverables for submission and summit use.
