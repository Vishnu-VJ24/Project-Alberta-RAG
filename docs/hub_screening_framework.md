# Recreation Hub Screening Framework

Date: 2026-06-26

## Purpose

This framework converts the Week 1 source review into a repeatable method for screening possible tourism and recreation hubs along the Bow and Red Deer River corridors.

## Screening Criteria

| Criterion | What It Measures | Why It Matters | Suggested Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| TDZ alignment | Whether the hub fits one or more Travel Alberta Tourism Development Zones | Keeps recommendations aligned with provincial destination development priorities | 1-5 |
| Corridor access | Highway, airport, rail, transit, shuttle, and last-mile access | Tourism hubs need realistic visitor movement, not just attractive assets | 1-5 |
| Activity fit | Match with activities identified by Travel Alberta such as hiking, camping, cabins, wildlife viewing, farmers' markets, breweries, heritage, or dinosaurs | Helps connect hub ideas to visitor demand signals | 1-5 |
| Economic potential | Jobs, visitor spend growth, tourism spend growth, and business development opportunity | Helps prioritize hubs with measurable economic-development value | 1-5 |
| Rural benefit | Ability to support small communities, local businesses, and visitor dispersal | Addresses geographic imbalance and spreads tourism benefits outside legacy destinations | 1-5 |
| Seasonality potential | Ability to support spring, fall, or winter visitation | Addresses seasonal compression in Alberta tourism | 1-5 |
| Stakeholder readiness | Community support, business engagement, and local partner readiness | Improves implementation feasibility | 1-5 |
| RAG evidence strength | Quality and quantity of source material available for the hub | Ensures final recommendations can be explained and cited by the RAG system | 1-5 |

## Initial Scoring Approach

Use a 1-5 score for each criterion.

- 1 = weak fit or little evidence
- 2 = limited fit
- 3 = moderate fit
- 4 = strong fit
- 5 = very strong fit with clear source support

The first version of the hub score can use a simple unweighted total. Later versions can weight criteria after stakeholder priorities are clearer.

## Example Use

If a proposed hub has strong TDZ alignment, strong corridor access, clear visitor activities, and strong source support, it should move into deeper analysis. If a hub has attractive assets but weak access or weak evidence, it should remain a future opportunity rather than a core recommendation.

## RAG Connection

Each candidate hub should be connected to source chunks in the RAG corpus:

- TDZ source page
- Tourism indicators
- Municipal plans
- Transportation corridor sources
- Geospatial asset notes
- Economic model assumptions
- Screenshots and chart captions

This makes the RAG system useful for explaining why a hub was prioritized, what evidence supports it, and where uncertainty remains.
